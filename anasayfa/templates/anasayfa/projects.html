THE PROBLEM OF IMPROVING ACCESSIBILITY AND PLACEMENT OF SMART DELIVERY LOCKERS IN ESKIŞEHIR TEPEBAŞI DISTRICT

This is a group project carried out as part of the Bachelor's Graduation Project. The aim is to present a comprehensive decision support system that aims to determine the most suitable locations for smart delivery lockers among 21 urban neighborhoods in the Eskişehir/Tepebaşı district using the Set Covering Problem and the TOPSIS method. The solution process includes prioritizing neighborhoods based on their demographic characteristics using the TOPSIS method, calculating distances between neighborhoods to create a coverage matrix, and using the Set Covering optimization model to maximize coverage with the minimum number of lockers and to prioritize the selected centers. Through a visual and interactive HTML interface, users can select a coverage radius, and based on this selection, the system automatically performs the optimization. The Google Street View images of the selected centers are provided just one click away, ensuring a user-friendly and practical structure. The results are presented both in table and map formats, offering a dynamic and integrated solution that provides decision-makers with accessibility-, efficiency-, and priority-based location suggestions.

My individual roles in this project included drawing neighborhood boundaries and converting geographic coordinates into a format processable by the model using ScribbleMaps and QGIS, creating visuals for the interface and the project fair poster using Adobe Photoshop, performing data analysis, developing the optimization model and coding structure using Python, and developing a Decision Support System with an HTML-based interface.

EnerjiSA - SPARK'24: PRINCIPLES OF PARALLEL OPERATION OF POWER TRANSFORMERS IN DISTRIBUTION NETWORKS

Between the dates of 02.07.2024 - 30.07.2024, within the scope of my Mandatory Management Internship at EnerjiSA - Toroslar EDAŞ, this includes the parts I was responsible for and the work I carried out during the project we conducted together with my group members and our group coach Ahmet CANBOLAT. The objective was to determine whether temperature and humidity levels have an effect on the observed peak values of 14 step-down substations located in the Mersin Metropolitan Area in line with the principles of parallel connection of power transformers in distribution networks, and to provide prediction opportunities and analyze the results. In this context, data on daily peak values and transformer capacities, daily average temperature values, and daily average humidity rates between 01.01.2023 and 30.06.2024 were used. In this context, data covering daily peak values and transformer capacities, daily average temperature values, and daily average humidity rates between 01.01.2023 and 30.06.2024 were used.

My individual roles in this project included retrieving the data and preparing it for processing, performing regression analyses in Python to examine the effects of temperature and humidity levels on peak values, deriving regression equations for each transformer, and developing a Parameter-Based Power Predictor using these equations. Additionally, energy efficiency calculations were carried out based on the prediction results, critical transformers were identified through Pareto analysis according to the criteria of average active power and average occupancy rate, and the entire process was visually presented along with the preparation of a project introduction video.

ENM326 - Network Optimization and Algorithms: NURSE STAFF SCHEDULING

This study was carried out as part of the ENM326 - Network Optimization and Algorithms course. In the project, a maximum flow optimization model was developed by considering nurse requirements for different shifts and departments, as well as capacity limits and demands. In the model, flow variables between shifts and departments were defined, upper and lower bounds and demand constraints were introduced, and an objective function was formulated to minimize the total number of nurses. The model was solved using Gurobi, the obtained optimal flow values were interpreted, and the results were visualized in layers through a detailed network graph. Thus, the allocation of nurses to shifts and departments was optimized, enabling efficient use of resources.

In this project, I was responsible for carrying out the coding process, preparing the report, and creating the presentation. I also undertook the tasks of conducting network simulations and performing performance analyses.

ENM310 - Design of Experiments and Regression Analysis: TEA - TEMPERATURE - TIME

Within the scope of the ENM310 - Design of Experiments and Regression Analysis course, a project was conducted to examine the effects of brewing tea under different conditions on taste. Three different factors—type of tea, water temperature, and brewing time—and their effects at two levels were analyzed. The taste score was considered as the output variable, and evaluations were made using statistical methods such as analysis of variance (ANOVA), interaction plots, and the Tukey test. According to the findings, all three factors had statistically significant effects on the taste of the tea; however, the interactions between these factors were found to be insignificant. Through this project, I had the opportunity to enhance my theoretical knowledge and analytical skills in experimental design and multi-factor data analysis.

In this project, my roles included preparing the presentation, performing the necessary statistical method calculations, and interpreting the results.
